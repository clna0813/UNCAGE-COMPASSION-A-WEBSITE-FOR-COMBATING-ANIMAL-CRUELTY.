animals face cruelty every day. This website is dedicated to raising

awareness and taking action to end animal cruelty in all its forms.

What is Animal Cruelty?

Animal cruelty, also known as animal abuse or neglect, is any act that inflicts suffering or harm on an animal. This can be done intentionally, through violence or neglect, or unintentionally, through a lack of understanding of an animal's needs.

Two Main Forms of Animal Cruelty:

• Active cruelty: This is the intentional infliction of pain or suffering, such as beating, kicking, or starving an animal.

• Passive cruelty: This is the neglect of an animal's basic needs, such as food, water, shelter, and veterinary care.

Signs that an animal might be suffering from cruelty:

• Physical signs: Thinness, malnutrition, dehydration, wounds, sores, matted fur, excessive hair loss

• Behavioral signs: Fearfulness, aggression, lethargy, excessive vocalization, pacing

A Story of a Human helping the Animal in Need

Kai, a young surfer with hair the color of sun-bleached sand, wasn't just at the beach for the waves. He spent his afternoons scouring the coastline, a net bag slung over his shoulder, collecting the plastic debris that occasionally washed ashore. One breezy afternoon, a flash of emerald green caught his eye. Half-buried in the sand, trapped in a tangled mess of fishing line, was a green sea turtle. Panic surged through Kai. He scrambled towards the creature, careful not to startle it. The turtle thrashed in the plastic prison, its flippers churning uselessly. With practiced efficiency, Kai knelt beside it. He spoke in soothing tones, a calming mantra he'd picked up from his marine biologist grandfather. Slowly, meticulously, he began untangling the line. Patience was key – a single wrong tug could injure the already stressed turtle. Minutes ticked by, the sun dipping lower towards the horizon. Finally, with a triumphant snap, the last knot gave way. The turtle, free at last, let out a low gurgle. Kai watched, his heart swelling, as it lumbered towards the water. Just as it reached the edge of the surf, the turtle turned its head, as if in acknowledgement. For a fleeting moment, their eyes met – a silent exchange between a grateful creature and its unlikely savior. A grin stretched across Kai's face. He knew this wasn't a one-time thing. As long as the ocean needed him, he'd be there, a guardian angel with a net bag and a heart full of respect for the creatures who shared his world. The waves could wait. Today, Kai had made a difference, and that feeling, he knew, was a wave worth riding.
